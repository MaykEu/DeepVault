# DeepVault Guide

Welcome to DeepVault — an interactive learning fortress for mastering C++, Unreal Engine, and the computer systems beneath them.

## How This Works

Every folder on the dashboard contains **Learn** notes and **Quiz** exercises:

| Mode | What It Does |
|---|---|
| **Learn** | The full vault note, rendered exactly as it appears in Obsidian. Read, scroll the right-side table of contents, click wiki links to jump between notes. Password-protected. |
| **Quiz** | 5-10 randomized questions per note — multiple choice and text input. Instant feedback. Scores saved locally on your device. |

### Tracking Your Progress

Progress is based on **quiz scores only** — reading a note does not count. Here's how it works:

| Status | What It Means |
|---|---|
| **NOT STARTED** | You haven't taken the quiz yet |
| **IN PROGRESS** | At least 1 attempt, but best score is below 80% |
| **COMPLETED** | You scored 80% or higher on at least one attempt |

The folder header shows your overall completion percentage (completedNotes / totalNotes). Quiz scores and attempt history are saved locally via localStorage — they never leave your device.

**Wiki links** like [[CPU Architecture]] connect notes to each other. Click any link to jump to that note's Learn page. Links to notes not yet available show "(coming soon)".

**Recent notes** appear on the dashboard below your folders — quick jump back to what you were studying.

## Master Study Order

This vault is designed to be read in order. Each note builds on the ones before it.

### Layer 1 — Computer Systems (start here)

Without understanding the hardware, C++ is just magic words. Start with the physical reality.

1. [[Bits, Bytes & Number Systems]] — What a bit physically IS
2. [[Negative Numbers & Endianness]] — Two's complement, signed vs unsigned
3. [[Assembly Language]] — The bridge between C++ and hardware
4. [[CPU Architecture]] — Fetch-decode-execute, pipelining, branch prediction
5. [[Memory Hierarchy]] — Cache lines, L1/L2/L3, why RAM is slow
6. [[RAM & Virtual Memory]] — Page tables, TLB, mmap, swap
7. [[Virtual Memory — The Full Picture]] — Segmentation, page replacement, Linux reclamation
8. [[Memory Allocation — From malloc to Buddy System]] — malloc internals, buddy system, slab, custom allocators
9. [[Storage]] — HDD vs SSD, filesystems, page cache
10. [[RAID]] — Striping, mirroring, parity, ZFS, the write hole
11. [[Function Calls & Stack Frames]] — How call and ret actually work
12. [[Compiler Pipeline]] — Preprocessor to assembler to linker
13. [[Linking & Executables]] — Static vs dynamic linking, DLLs
14. [[Syscalls & Kernel]] — Protection rings, syscall instruction, futex
15. [[Scheduling — MLFQ & Proportional Share]] — MLFQ, lottery/stride scheduling, CFS, EEVDF
16. [[Threads & Processes]] — Scheduler, context switching, thread pools
17. [[Concurrency & Synchronization]] — Mutexes, atomics, memory ordering, lock-free and lock-based structures
18. [[Condition Variables & Semaphores]] — Waiting rooms, producer-consumer, barriers, POSIX semaphores
19. [[Deadlock — The Four Conditions]] — Coffman conditions, prevention, priority inversion
20. [[GPU Architecture Overview]] — SIMT, VRAM, compute shaders

Use the [[Learning Path]] for a detailed walkthrough with phase-by-phase guidance.
